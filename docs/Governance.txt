## 1. Purpose and Scope

This section defines the governance boundary of the Hermes GenAI KnowledgeBase system and establishes the scope within which governance controls apply.It clarifies the responsibilities, operational boundaries, and control domains governed by this framework.

1.1 Control Objective


- The governance frameworkSHALL define and enforce the operational boundary of Hermes to ensure regulatory clarity,and that accountability, risk ownership and access control decision are explicitly assigned, reviewable and auditable.

1.2 Scope of Governance

- This governance document applies only to the AI generated KnowledgeBase system, Hermes.
- The framework SHALL govern runtime behavior, access control enforcement, retrieval constraints, generation guardrails, logging, and oversight mechanisms.
- Hermes SHALL enforce governance controls at request time prior to any retrieval or generation.

1.3 Explicitly Out of Scope
- HermesSHALL NOT be responsible for upstream data validation, data classification accuracy, or governance controls implemented by source systems. 
- Upstream systems SHALL maintain independent governance and control frameworks.
- Generated outputSHALL NOT be persistently stored beyond runtime execution unless governed by an approved retention policy.

1.4 Boundary of Responsibility

- The system owner SHALL be accountable for governance oversight and risk acceptance relating to Hermes.
- Platform operations manager SHALL be responsible for operational enforcement of governance controls defined in this document.
 - Governance policies defined in this frameworkSHALL NOT be modified or overridden at runtime by system components or AI models.

## 2. Governance Principles

The governance principles define a set of guidelines under which Hermes is designed, operated and oversighted. These principles are independents of the platform and are applied regardless of the underlying technology that’s implemented.

2.1 Zero-Trust Architecture
Hermes SHALL operate under zero trust security model. TrustSHALL NOT be implicitly granted to any user, request or system components.

-Every request SHALL include verified identity context.
-Any access SHALL require explicit authorization.
-Retrieval SHALL be constrained by authorization policy.
-Generation SHALL be limited to authorized retrieved context and approved system instructions


2.2 Least Privilege Enforcement
Hermes SHALL enforce least privilege access control.

Access SHALL only be granted when all the following condition are staisfied:
- Requesting identity is authorized to view the information.
- Access is explicitly permitted by policy rules.
- The request context requires access to the information.

Authorization SHALL be enforced at the classified content chunk level and SHALL NOT rely solely on document-level access controls.

2.3 Deterministic Access Control

All access decisions SHALL be deterministic and reproducible. 


Authorization decisionsSHALL be based solely on:
-Authoritative identity context.
-Defined policy rules.
-Metadata classification associated with requested content. 

AI modelsSHALL NOT participate in access control or authorization decisions.

AccessSHALL be denied when authorization cannot be deterministically established.


2.4 Guarded Generation
Hermes' generation SHALL be constrained to authorized retrieved context.


- Generation SHALL rely only on authorized retrieved content.
- Generation SHALL be governed by approved system instructions.
- Generation SHALL comply with defined response guardrails.

Responses SHALL remain grounded in authorized retrived sources.

Generation SHALL NOT rely on model inference beyond authorized context.

2.5 Explicit Denial and Fail-Closed Default

Hermes SHALL operate under a fail-closed security model.

Access SHALL be denied when any of the following conditions occur:
-identity validation fails
-Authorization can not be resolved deterministically.
-Policy evaluation is inconclusive
-retrieval constraints are violated

The denial response SHALL NOT revewl the existence of the protected data or any internal policy logic behind this behaviors.

2.6 Separation of Governance and Runtime Control

Governance SHALL define the policies and control requirements that Hermes must enforce.

Runtime components SHALL enforce the rules set by the governance and SHALL NOT redefine or create new policy rules.

This separation ensures clear accountability and auditability of policy definition and enforcement.


2.7 Accountability and Auditability

All system access and authorization decisions at all levels SHALL be logged. 

Logs SHALL be traceable to the originating identity and request context.

Logging SHALL support at least one of these objectives:
-Accountability
-Security and Policy Compliance
-Investigation
-Improvement

Logs SHALL avoid storing sensitive data unless required for approved security or audit purposes.

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

The governance structure of Hermes establishes clear accountability, defensible decision-making, and separation of duties.

3.1 System Owner(Accountable)
The system owners SHALL be accountable fr the business capabilities delivered by Hermes.

Responsibility includes:

- Accountability for the business capabilities delivered by Hermes
- Acceptance of governance and operational risks
- Ensuring alignment with the enterprise risk management framework
- Escalating material risks or governance concerns to executive leadership

The System Owner SHALL NOT directly implement or modify governance controls.

3.2 Security and Access Control Owner (Accountable)

The Security and Access Control Owner SHALL be accountable for the integrity and effectiveness of Hermes access control standards and authorization policies.

Responsibilities include:

- Defining and maintaining access control standards and authorization requirements.
- Reviewing policy integrity and access control effectiveness on a scheduled basis.
- Monitoring misuse and anomaly signals relating to access enforcement.
- Approving changes to the access model jointly with relevant Data Owners.

The Security and Access Control Owner SHALL NOT unilaterally define or modify data classifications, which remain under Data Owner accountability.

3.3 Data Owners (Accountable)

Each data domain integrated into Hermes SHALL have a designated Data Owner accountable for data classification, entitlement definition, and access risk within their domain.

Responsibilities include:

• Ensuring accurate data classification and sensitivity labeling for content made available to Hermes
• Defining and maintaining entitlement mappings for their data domain
• Providing formal approval for access rules and policy changes that impact their data domain
• Performing periodic access recertification at defined intervals to validate continued access needs

Data Owners SHALL provide individual, formal approval for any change that alters data exposure risk within their domain.


3.4 Platform Engineering(Responsible)
Platform engineer SHALL be responisble for :
- Implementing approved governance controls and enforcement mechanisms.
- Ensuring platform reliability, operational resilience, and availability of Hermes.
- Ensuring fail-closed enforcement operates correctly under failure conditions.

Platform Engineering SHALL NOT approve policy changes or override governance controls.

3.5 Risk and Governance Committee (Oversight)

The Risk and Governance Committee SHALL provide independent oversight of Hermes governance and risk posture.

The Committee SHALL:

- Review and endorse material policy and access model changes.
- Review governance effectiveness metrics and control performance signals.
- Receive and review risk posture reporting and material risk exceptions.
- Provide oversight of material incidents and ensure corrective actions are tracked to closure.


The Committee SHALL ensure independent review and challenge of governance decisions beyond operational teams.

The Committee SHALL escalate material governance concerns and risk exceptions to the Executive Sponsor where required.

3.6 Role Mapping Summary (Informative) 

Basic overview of roles and responsibilities
System Owner: Chief Information Officer (CIO).
Executive Sponsor: Chief Risk Officer (CRO).
Oversight:  Risk & Governance Committee.(AI Risk & Ethics Committee)
Change Approval: Formal Change Advisory Board (CAB).
Incident Response: Security Operations + Compliance + Legal.

## 4. Access and Policy Governance


4.1 Policy Definition

AI components SHALL NOT define or infer the access rules.

All access rules SHALL be explicitly documented.
Policy evaluation SHALL be deterministic and reproducible.
Policy SHALL be traceable to an accountable owner.

Access to content SHALL be based solely on authoritative identity claims and approved metadata classification.

4.2 Policy change management

- All policy changes SHALL be documented with a business justification.
- All policy changes SHALL includes a documented Risk-impact assessment aligned with applicable regularation and enterprise risk requiremnts.
- Policy change approvals SHALL be recorded and include at minimum approval from Security and the relevent Data Owners.
- All policy changes SHALL require version-control and include change records sufficient to reproduce the policy state at any point in time.
- Material policy changes SHALL require Risk and Governance Committee review and endorsement.
- Emergency changes to policy MAY be implement to restore serurity or service continuty but SHALL be retrospectively reviewed and formally approved within a defined timeframe.

- Material changes SHALL include modifications that alter access scope, data exposure risk, enforcement logic, or governance control effectiveness.

4.3 Access Recertifaction/Changes

- Access entitlements SHALL be reviewed at defined intervals.
- Any recertification for access entitlement SHALL be conducted by data owners for each data domain.
- Recetification SHALL be triggered at minimum upon role changes, job/title change or policy change that affect entitlement scope.
- Privilege drift SHALL be monitored reported and corrected to maintatin least privilege access.
- Entitlements that cannot be recertified SHALL be removed or suspended until recertification is completed.
